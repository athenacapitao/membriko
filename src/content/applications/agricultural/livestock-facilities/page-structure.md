# Livestock Facilities — Page Structure

## Strategic Rationale

Livestock facilities is a high-value, regulation-driven application for Membriko. Unlike roofing
or decorative applications, this market segment is driven by legal compliance: Portuguese farms
above defined thresholds must demonstrate watertight effluent containment. The audience is
therefore a mix of farm owners under regulatory pressure, agricultural engineers specifying
compliance solutions, and DRAPs reviewing licensing applications.

The page must accomplish four objectives simultaneously:
1. Establish Membriko as the technical authority for EPDM in agricultural effluent containment
2. Explain the regulatory obligation (many farmers do not fully understand what DL 81/2013
   requires of them)
3. Cover all livestock facility types — from lagoons to drinking troughs to biogas digesters
4. Provide enough chemical and engineering detail to satisfy specifiers and licensing reviewers

The persuasion arc differs from roofing: here the primary motivation is compliance-first, then
ROI. The reader is often already convinced they need a solution — they need to know Membriko
is the right supplier and EPDM is the right material.

Section order:
1. Introduction — stakes (compliance, environmental protection, farm viability)
2. Why EPDM for livestock — chemical resistance, longevity, regulatory acceptance
3. Facility types — comprehensive coverage of all livestock waterproofing scenarios
4. Chemical resistance — deep technical section on ammonia, slurry, H₂S
5. Installation process — step-by-step, professional, trustworthy
6. Effluent containment regulations — what the law actually requires
7. EPDM vs HDPE vs concrete — handle the alternatives objection
8. Environmental compliance — Nitrates Directive, NREAP, broader context
9. Technical specifications — data for specifiers
10. Maintenance — lifetime support positioning
11. Portuguese livestock regulations — DRAP, licensing, practical guidance
12. Biogas integration — growth opportunity, Membriko capability
13. Health and safety — H₂S, ammonia, confined spaces — responsible messaging
14. FAQs — 8 questions capturing regulatory and technical queries
15. CTA — conversion with compliance urgency

## Section 4: Resistência Química — EPDM vs Amónia e Chorume

**Purpose:** The deepest technical credibility signal in this page. Target specifiers and
licensing reviewers who need to see documented chemical resistance.

**Content:**
- Molecular explanation of EPDM's chemical inertness (saturated backbone, no ester bonds)
- NH₃ resistance data: ASTM D471 immersion results, <5% property change
- H₂S resistance: good performance at agricultural concentrations (< 500 ppm)
- Slurry pH tolerance: continuous service at pH 4-12 validated
- Comparison with concrete: calcium silicate hydrate attacked by ammonium (NH₄⁺) → calcium
  nitrate, progressive porosity, concrete weakening
- Comparison with HDPE: good chemical resistance but susceptible to UV and biological
  oxidation; harder to repair; no elongation to bridge substrate cracks
- Comparison with bitumen membranes: rapid degradation in alkaline slurry environments;
  not rated for permanent immersion

## Section 5: Processo de Instalação em Instalações Pecuárias

**Purpose:** Demonstrate professional competence and de-risk the purchase decision.

**Content — 6-step installation process:**

1. **Diagnóstico e Projeto**
   - Site survey: substrate condition, soil type, water table depth
   - Volume calculation per DL 81/2013 requirements
   - Membrane specification (thickness, formulation for chemical environment)
   - Geotextile specification based on soil conditions

2. **Preparação do Substrato**
   - Compaction and levelling of earth bases (≥ 95% Proctor)
   - Removal of stones, roots, sharp objects
   - Concrete substrate preparation: cleaning, crack repair, edge chamfering
   - Geotextile 300 g/m² installation for protection on earth bases

3. **Instalação da Membrana EPDM**
   - Panel layout optimised to minimise seams
   - Minimum 200 mm overlap at all seams
   - Factory-vulcanised seam tape for field joins
   - Penetration detailing: outlets, inlets, service pipes

4. **Ancoragem Perimetral**
   - Anchor trench at lagoon perimeter (minimum 300 mm depth)
   - Mechanical fixing with stainless steel bar on concrete structures
   - Uplift resistance calculation for biogas and wind pressure

5. **Cobertura Flutuante (Opcional)**
   - Floating EPDM cover panel installation
   - Perimeter seal to prevent bypass of gas
   - Collection point for gas off-take
   - Access points for inspection and pump operation

6. **Teste de Estanquidade e Documentação**
   - Hydraulic integrity test (water fill and level monitoring)
   - Photographic documentation of all installation stages
   - Issue of installation certificate for PGEP/DRAP submission
   - 15-year Membriko guarantee

## Section 13: Saúde e Segurança em Instalações Pecuárias

**Purpose:** Responsible messaging — demonstrate awareness of H₂S and ammonia risks.

**Content:**
- H₂S: produced by anaerobic decomposition in lagoons; extremely hazardous at > 50 ppm;
  IDLH 50 ppm; immediately dangerous at 100 ppm; heavier than air (collects at low points)
- NH₃: irritating at > 25 ppm; toxic at > 300 ppm; risk in enclosed pig buildings
- Safe working procedures during membrane installation: confined space entry protocols;
  atmospheric monitoring; rescue arrangements
- EPDM installation reduces risk compared with concrete construction: no open-face
  lagoon during construction period; quicker installation window
- Post-installation: EPDM floating covers reduce atmospheric NH₃ by 70-90%,
  improving occupational environment for farm workers
- Compliance with Portaria 243/2011 (farm worker health and safety obligations)
