## 2. Why EPDM Is the Ideal Green Roof Membrane

### Natural Root Resistance — No Biocides Required

The defining advantage of EPDM for green roofs is its inherent resistance to root penetration. EPDM is a vulcanised synthetic rubber with a densely cross-linked molecular structure. The three-dimensional network of carbon-carbon bonds creates a physical barrier that root tips cannot penetrate — no chemical treatment, no biocidal additives, no degradation over time.

This is a critical distinction from competing membrane types:
- **PVC membranes** require mecoprop or pendimethalin biocides incorporated into the formulation to resist roots. These biocides leach into drainage water over the membrane's life — creating an environmental contradiction on a system designed to improve urban ecology.
- **SBS modified bitumen** cannot resist roots at all without a separate root barrier layer (typically copper foil or HDPE sheet), adding cost, complexity and another potential failure interface.
- **TPO membranes** also require biocidal treatment for root resistance certification.

EPDM stands alone as the only single-ply membrane that achieves root resistance through its fundamental chemistry rather than chemical additives.

### FLL Certification and EN 13948

The FLL (Forschungsgesellschaft Landschaftsentwicklung Landschaftsbau) root penetration test is the internationally recognised standard for green roof membrane qualification. Codified as EN 13948, the test protocol exposes the membrane to aggressive root species (Agropyron repens and Quercus robur) for a minimum of 24 months under controlled growing conditions. The membrane must show zero evidence of root penetration, perforation or deformation.

EPDM consistently passes this test with clear margins. Membriko uses exclusively FLL-certified EPDM, ensuring every square metre installed beneath a green roof is proven to resist root intrusion for the full service life of the system.

### Permanent Moisture Resistance

A green roof membrane lives in permanent contact with moisture. The growing substrate retains water by design; the drainage layer may maintain a water film; winter rainfall saturates the entire assembly for months at a time. EPDM is chemically inert to water — it does not undergo hydrolysis, plasticiser migration or dimensional change in wet conditions.

PVC membranes, by contrast, progressively lose their plasticiser content in permanently wet environments, becoming rigid and brittle over 15-20 years. This is precisely the environment of a green roof, making PVC a technically questionable choice for this application despite its lower initial cost.

## 3. Green Roof Types: Extensive, Semi-Intensive and Intensive

### Extensive Green Roofs
- **Substrate depth**: 60-150 mm
- **Saturated weight**: 60-150 kg/m²
- **Vegetation**: Sedum species, mosses, drought-tolerant grasses
- **Maintenance**: 1-2 visits per year after establishment
- **EPDM specification (Membriko)**: 1.2 mm minimum thickness
- **Best for**: Retrofit on existing buildings, large commercial/industrial areas, stormwater compliance

### Semi-Intensive Green Roofs
- **Substrate depth**: 150-300 mm
- **Saturated weight**: 150-300 kg/m²
- **Vegetation**: Perennial grasses, herbs, low shrubs
- **Maintenance**: 3-6 visits per year
- **EPDM specification (Membriko)**: 1.5 mm minimum
- **Best for**: Corporate buildings, schools, mixed-use developments seeking visible greenery

### Intensive Green Roofs (Rooftop Gardens)
- **Substrate depth**: 300 mm to 1,000+ mm
- **Saturated weight**: 300-1,200 kg/m²
- **Vegetation**: Shrubs, trees, lawns, full garden planting
- **Maintenance**: Regular garden maintenance schedule
- **EPDM specification (Membriko)**: 1.5-2.0 mm with protection board overlay
- **Best for**: Luxury residential, hotels, public buildings with accessible roof terraces

## 4. The Layer System Above EPDM

A professional green roof is built in nine layers, from bottom to top:

1. **Structural deck** — reinforced concrete slab engineered for green roof loads
2. **Vapour control layer** — in warm-roof configurations where insulation sits above the slab
3. **Thermal insulation** — XPS or PIR boards with adequate compressive strength
4. **EPDM waterproofing membrane** — FLL-certified, root-resistant, fully adhered
5. **Protection geotextile** — non-woven fabric (300-500 g/m²) protecting EPDM during construction
6. **Drainage layer** — expanded clay aggregate (30-60 mm) or moulded drainage boards
7. **Filter geotextile** — prevents fine substrate particles migrating into the drainage layer
8. **Growing substrate** — engineered mineral-based medium (NOT topsoil)
9. **Vegetation** — pre-grown Sedum mats, plant plugs or seed mix

The critical design principle is that the EPDM membrane must be flawless before any layer is placed above it. Once the drainage, substrate and vegetation are installed, accessing the membrane for repair requires removing everything — a process that can cost more than the original green roof installation.

Membriko's single-sheet EPDM capability is particularly valuable here. Sheets up to 15.25 m wide by 61 m long can cover large roof areas with zero field seams. Every seam eliminated is one fewer potential root penetration point and one fewer potential leak source buried under tonnes of substrate.

## 5. Stormwater Management with Green Roofs

### Retention and Attenuation Data

Green roofs are quantifiable stormwater infrastructure:

- **Annual retention**: Extensive systems retain 40-60% of annual rainfall; intensive systems 60-80%
- **Peak flow attenuation**: For moderate events (<20 mm), green roofs reduce peak runoff by 60-90%
- **Peak delay**: Runoff peak is typically delayed by 30-90 minutes versus conventional roofs
- **Water quality**: Reduced suspended solids and certain pollutant concentrations in runoff

### Portuguese Rainfall Context

For **Lisbon** (average annual rainfall ~725 mm, concentrated October-March), an extensive green roof can retain approximately 300-400 litres per m² annually — equivalent to removing 30-40% of the roof's contribution to the storm sewer system.

For **Porto** (average annual rainfall ~1,200 mm), absolute retention volumes are higher but percentage retention is somewhat lower due to the more intense and prolonged wet season.

These figures are increasingly relevant as Portuguese municipalities tighten stormwater management requirements for new development. Membriko provides detailed retention calculations for each project, supporting clients' compliance documentation for planning approvals.

## 6. Membriko's 8-Step Green Roof Installation Process

### Step 1: Structural Load Assessment
Verification of slab load capacity. An extensive green roof requires minimum 150 kg/m² additional saturated load capacity; intensive systems may require 500-1,200 kg/m². Membriko coordinates with the structural engineer before proceeding.

### Step 2: Surface Preparation and Falls
The slab must have minimum 1-2% falls for effective drainage. Where necessary, Membriko forms lightweight screed falls before membrane installation.

### Step 3: EPDM Membrane Installation
Application of FLL-certified root-resistant EPDM, fully adhered to the substrate. For green roofs, Membriko specifies full adhesion rather than mechanical fastening to eliminate any possibility of water tracking between membrane and substrate.

### Step 4: Flood Testing
Before any layer is placed above the membrane, Membriko conducts a 48-hour flood test. This is a non-negotiable quality gate — any leak identified at this stage is simple and inexpensive to repair. After green roof layers are installed, the same repair could cost ten times more.

### Step 5: Protection Geotextile
Installation of non-woven geotextile (300-500 g/m²) over the EPDM. This layer protects the membrane from puncture during placement of drainage material and substrate.

### Step 6: Drainage Layer
Placement of expanded clay aggregate (Leca or equivalent, 30-60 mm depth) or proprietary moulded drainage boards. Membriko sizes drainage capacity to the regional rainfall profile.

### Step 7: Filter Geotextile and Substrate
Application of filter fabric and spreading of engineered mineral growing substrate. The substrate is NOT garden soil — it is a specifically formulated medium with >80% mineral content to ensure drainage, aeration and structural stability under saturation.

### Step 8: Planting and Establishment
Installation of vegetation (pre-grown Sedum mats for extensive, individual planting for semi-intensive and intensive). Irrigation system configuration where applicable. Establishment period of 12-18 months with enhanced maintenance.

## 7. Mediterranean Climate Adaptation

### The Portuguese Summer Challenge

Most green roof systems and guidelines were developed in Northern and Central Europe, where rainfall is distributed throughout the year and summer temperatures are moderate. Portugal's Mediterranean climate demands adaptation:

- **Summer drought**: The Algarve receives less than 5% of its annual rainfall between June and September. Even drought-tolerant Sedum species require supplementary irrigation during the establishment period and during extreme drought spells.
- **Extreme heat**: Substrate surface temperatures can exceed 50-60°C. The EPDM membrane beneath is protected by the substrate's thermal mass but must withstand sustained temperatures of 40-50°C — comfortably within its -45°C to +130°C service range.
- **Winter deluge**: Intense rainfall events exceeding 50 mm/day (occasionally 100+ mm/day) require generous drainage layer capacity and emergency overflow provisions.

### Plant Species for Portuguese Green Roofs

Membriko recommends species proven in Mediterranean conditions:

**Extensive systems**: Sedum album, S. hispanicum (native to Iberia), S. sediforme (quintessentially Mediterranean), Delosperma cooperi (intense flowering, heat-tolerant)

**Semi-intensive systems**: Lavandula stoechas (French lavender), Thymus vulgaris (thyme), Armeria maritima (sea thrift — ideal for coastal conditions), Rosmarinus officinalis (rosemary)

Research from ISA/Universidade de Lisboa and ISEP/Porto has validated these species selections for Portuguese green roof applications, with Sedum hispanicum showing particularly strong performance in Lisbon's climate.

## 8. Environmental and Energy Benefits

### Urban Heat Island Reduction
Green roofs reduce roof surface temperature by 20-40°C compared to conventional dark roofs. In Lisbon, where the urban heat island effect can add 3-5°C to ambient temperatures in summer, widespread green roof adoption would make a measurable contribution to urban cooling.

### Building Energy Performance
- **Summer cooling reduction**: 10-25% lower cooling energy demand
- **Winter heating reduction**: 5-10% improvement (additional insulation effect)
- **Combined with EPDM**: The membrane + insulation + substrate assembly creates a multi-layer thermal buffer

### Carbon Sequestration
Extensive green roofs sequester approximately 1.5-2.5 kg CO₂/m²/year. Intensive systems with shrub planting can achieve 3-5 kg CO₂/m²/year. Over a 50-year membrane life, this represents meaningful cumulative carbon capture.

### Biodiversity
A well-designed green roof creates habitat for pollinators, birds and micro-organisms. In Portugal's urban centres, green roofs serve as ecological stepping stones connecting parks, gardens and natural areas.

### Acoustic Performance
The green roof system provides 8-10 dB noise reduction — significant for buildings near roads, railways or flight paths.

## 14. Frequently Asked Questions

### How much weight does a green roof add to the structure?
An extensive green roof when saturated typically weighs 80-150 kg/m², comparable to a traditional tile roof. Intensive green roofs (rooftop gardens) can reach 300-1,200 kg/m² and require specific structural engineering. Membriko always assesses structural capacity as the first step in any green roof project.

### Can a green roof be installed on an existing building?
Yes, provided the structure has sufficient load capacity. Lightweight extensive systems (80-100 kg/m²) are frequently viable on existing concrete-slab buildings. Membriko coordinates the structural assessment as the first project step.

### Does a green roof need irrigation in Portuguese summers?
For extensive Sedum-based systems adapted to Mediterranean conditions, irrigation is typically required during the first 12-18 months of establishment and during prolonged drought periods (more than 3 weeks without rain). Semi-intensive and intensive systems require regular summer irrigation.

### What happens if the EPDM membrane leaks under a green roof?
Repair requires localised removal of all layers above the membrane — vegetation, substrate, filter, drainage and protection fleece — to access the leak point. This is why Membriko conducts a 48-hour flood test BEFORE any upper layer is installed, and uses exclusively FLL-certified EPDM with a proven 50+ year service life.

### Can plant roots damage the EPDM membrane?
No. FLL/EN 13948-certified EPDM is proven to resist root penetration. Unlike PVC and bitumen membranes that require biocide treatment, EPDM resists roots naturally through its vulcanised cross-linked molecular structure.

### What does a green roof cost compared to a conventional roof?
An extensive green roof typically costs €80-150/m² installed (including EPDM membrane, drainage, substrate and vegetation), compared with €35-50/m² for a conventional flat roof with EPDM. The additional cost is recovered through energy savings, municipal incentives, property value enhancement and reduced stormwater management costs over the building's life.

### Can a green roof integrate solar panels?
Yes. Biosolar roof systems combine PV panels with green roof vegetation, using mounting frames that sit above the plant canopy. Membriko specifies EPDM-compatible mounting systems that do not compromise the waterproofing membrane.

### How long does a green roof with EPDM membrane last?
The EPDM membrane has a proven service life of 50+ years. The vegetation and substrate require periodic maintenance but are renewable without disturbing the membrane. The result is a system with a service life that exceeds most building use cycles.
