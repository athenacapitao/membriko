# Solar Panel Integration — Page Structure

## Strategic Rationale

Solar integration is a high-intent, high-value page that captures two intersecting audiences
simultaneously: building owners already committed to solar who need the right waterproofing
system, and building owners evaluating solar who have not yet considered the roof membrane
implications. Both arrive with commercial intent.

The page must establish a single primary conviction in the reader's mind before they contact
Membriko: that the choice of waterproofing membrane is the most consequential decision in any
rooftop solar project, because once the panels are installed, the membrane is inaccessible — and
if it fails before the panels' 25-30 year design life, the cost of replacement multiplies.
From this conviction, EPDM's 50+ year service life becomes the only rational choice.

The section order follows this persuasion logic:
1. Opportunity and stakes — solar is transforming Portuguese roofs; the membrane choice determines
   whether the investment succeeds or fails
2. Why EPDM is the optimal solar base — technical case before we describe our service
3. Mounting system options — technical confidence; we understand the hardware, not just the membrane
4. Waterproofing protection — how we ensure the membrane survives installation and operation
5. Portuguese solar market — regulatory context proves we navigate the full project, not just the roof
6. Installation process — de-risk the decision with a clear, predictable workflow
7. New build vs retrofit — address both audiences explicitly
8. Fire safety — compliance confidence for commercial and industrial clients
9. Maintenance under panels — address the "but how do you inspect it?" objection
10. Technical specifications — data for specifiers and engineers
11. EPDM vs alternatives — handle the "why not TPO/PVC/bitumen?" objection
12. Solar regulations — complete regulatory picture for autoconsumo projects
13. ROI analysis — rational financial case including avoided membrane replacement cost
14. FAQ — long-tail search and pre-sales query reduction

## Section 2: Por Que o EPDM É a Base Ideal para Painéis Solares

**Purpose:** Establish the technical superiority of EPDM for solar applications before any
discussion of mounting systems or Membriko's process.

**Content:** Five technical differentiators as a grid or list with brief explanations:

1. **Vida útil superior à dos painéis** — EPDM service life 50+ years vs 25-30 year panel
   design life; the membrane will never need replacing during the panel's operational period
   if correctly installed

2. **Compatibilidade química total** — EPDM is chemically inert to aluminium, stainless steel,
   galvanised steel, neoprene and EPDM isolation pads — all materials found in solar mounting
   systems; no galvanic or chemical degradation at contact points

3. **Estabilidade dimensional sob carga** — EPDM elastic recovery prevents creep deformation
   under sustained compressive loading from mount feet; ballasted systems rest on the membrane
   for decades without causing permanent indentation

4. **Instalação a frio — sem chama** — EPDM is installed cold (contact adhesive or self-adhered
   tape); no gas torch near cable conduits, junction boxes or other electrical infrastructure
   that may already be on the roof during installation or upgrade works

5. **Reparação sem remoção dos painéis** — EPDM seam repairs and minor damage repairs can be
   made with peel-and-stick EPDM tape without removing the mounting system in most cases;
   TPO and bitumen repairs require heat or torch, impossible near electrical equipment

**Visual:** Icon grid or feature cards. Side-by-side life expectancy bar chart (EPDM vs panel
design life vs competitor membranes).

## Section 3: Sistemas de Montagem Compatíveis — Ballast, Penetração e Híbrido

**Purpose:** Demonstrate expert knowledge of solar mounting hardware — not just the membrane.
This differentiates Membriko from roofers who know EPDM but not solar, and from solar installers
who know panels but not waterproofing.

**Content:** Three mounting approaches as cards or tabbed content:

**Sistemas de Lastro (Não Penetrantes)**
- Description: Aerodynamic ballasted trays (ValkBox, ValkAce, Schletter FlatFix) held by
  concrete ballast blocks; zero penetrations through the EPDM membrane
- How it works: Mount weight + aerodynamic profile resists wind uplift per EN 1991-1-4 calculation
- Ballast load: typically 15-25 kg/m² roof area (less than standard EPDM gravel ballast)
- Structural requirement: Engineer must verify existing roof slab can carry combined load
- EPDM interface: Isolation pads (EPDM or neoprene) prevent direct metal-to-membrane contact
- Best for: Most Portuguese flat roofs; avoids warranty impact on existing EPDM

**Sistemas de Fixação Penetrante (Estrutural)**
- Description: Bolted mounts anchored through membrane and insulation to structural deck
- Required when: wind uplift exceeds ballast capacity (coastal locations, tall buildings)
  or structural load capacity is insufficient for ballast
- EPDM treatment: Pre-fabricated EPDM boots and flange plates over each penetration,
  sealed with butyl tape and EPDM-compatible sealant; Membriko waterproofs every anchor
- Best for: High wind-exposure sites; roofs without structural capacity for ballast

**Sistemas Híbridos (Perímetro Fixo, Interior por Lastro)**
- Description: Mechanical fixings at perimeter rows; ballasted interior
- Wind uplift managed at most vulnerable roof edges; reduced ballast weight at interior
- Engineering calculation required for the combined loading pattern
- Best for: Large commercial roofs where ballast alone would exceed structural limits
  but full penetration is undesirable

**Visual:** Illustrated cross-section diagrams for each system type. Callout boxes for key
specifications (ballast weight, wind standard, EPDM treatment at contacts).

## Section 4: Impermeabilização Garantida Sob os Painéis

**Purpose:** Address the specific concern: once panels are installed, how do I know the
waterproofing remains intact?

**Content:**

**A Questão da Inacessibilidade**
Once solar panels are installed on ballasted mounts, the membrane beneath is partially or
wholly inaccessible to visual inspection. This makes pre-installation quality — the quality
of seams, singular points, drains and penetrations — more critical than on any other roof.
Membriko's response: treat every solar-destined roof as if it will never be visually inspected
again after panel installation.

**Quatro garantias Membriko de impermeabilização sob solar:**

1. **Teste de estanqueidade 100%** — Every solar-destined roof undergoes electronic leak detection
   or flood test before any mounting system is placed; no mounting proceeds over an untested
   membrane

2. **Pontos singulares em EPDM pré-formado** — All drains, upstands, penetrations and
   perimeter flashings use prefabricated EPDM accessories, not mastic or non-EPDM sealants
   that would degrade beneath the panels over a 25+ year period

3. **Registo fotográfico da membrana nua** — Photographic as-built record of the complete
   membrane surface before panel installation; timestamped and archived for warranty reference

4. **Corredor de drenagem garantido** — All Membriko solar roof layouts include panel row
   spacing that maintains functional drainage corridors; no panel placement that creates
   water retention zones

"Before panels" and "after panels" illustration showing the inspection timeline.

## Section 6: Processo de Instalação Membriko — 6 Etapas

**Purpose:** Make the combined solar waterproofing project feel predictable and professionally
managed. Reduce purchase anxiety for clients who have never done a solar roof project before.

**Content:** Numbered step timeline with icon per step.

1. **Avaliação Técnica da Cobertura** — Site visit with structural assessment review,
   measurement of roof area and slope, identification of all existing penetrations and
   equipment, photography of existing membrane condition (if present)

2. **Projeto de Impermeabilização Solar** — Written specification: membrane system, thickness,
   fastening method, all singular points; coordination with PV system designer for panel
   layout, row spacing, cable routing; fire setback plan per DL 220/2008; drainage design
   verified with panel layout

3. **Preparação do Suporte** — Substrate repair if required; removal of existing membrane
   if non-compatible; slope correction screeds; primer application

4. **Instalação da Membrana EPDM** — Membrane placement, bonding or fastening; all singular
   points (drains, upstands, penetrations) completed with prefabricated EPDM accessories;
   cable routing penetrations sealed with EPDM boots; full drainage function verified

5. **Teste de Estanqueidade e Registo** — 100% leak test before any mounting system is
   placed; photographic as-built record of complete membrane surface; test results
   documented for warranty file

6. **Coordenação com Instalador Solar** — Membriko issues clearance for mounting system
   installation; provides isolation pad specification to solar installer; available on-site
   during first day of mount installation to verify no membrane damage; final inspection
   of perimeter flashings after panel installation complete

**Visual:** Horizontal stepper component. Icon per step. Badge: "Coordenação Solar Incluída"
as a differentiator callout.

## Section 7: Obra Nova vs Retrofitting Solar — Abordagens Diferentes

**Purpose:** Address both audiences — new construction projects and existing buildings
adding solar to an existing roof.

**Content:** Two-column or two-card layout.

**Obra Nova com Solar Planeado**
- Membrane and solar system designed together from the start
- Optimal outcome: membrane spec, slope, drainage, penetrations and cable routes all
  coordinated before any work begins
- Membriko works with the project architect and PV engineer at design stage
- Cable management: conduit routes under insulation, emerging at planned penetration points
- Structural engineer involved from the start: membrane loads + solar loads designed together
- Timeline advantage: single mobilisation for membrane and mount installation (phased)

**Retrofit Solar em Cobertura Existente**
- Existing membrane must be assessed before any solar installation proceeds
- Membriko provides membrane condition survey: core samples if needed, visual inspection,
  identification of existing singular points and drainage
- Three outcomes from survey:
  a) Membrane in serviceable condition — proceed with solar; Membriko documents condition
     and provides solar-specific clearance; existing warranty implications must be reviewed
  b) Membrane requires targeted repairs — Membriko repairs specific defects, re-tests,
     then issues clearance for solar
  c) Membrane requires full replacement — Membriko replaces membrane before solar proceeds;
     best long-term outcome for the client
- Cost of membrane replacement amortised against 25-30 year solar investment

**Visual:** Decision flowchart for retrofit scenario. Timeline comparison for new build.

## Section 8: Segurança Contra Incêndio — Conformidade com DL 220/2008

**Purpose:** Compliance confidence for commercial and industrial clients. Fire safety
is frequently misunderstood in solar installations.

**Content:**

**Classificação de Reação ao Fogo**
- EPDM membrane: Class E (EN 13501-1) — the standard classification for single-ply
  synthetic rubber membranes
- Where BROOF(t4) is required (external fire performance): mineral ballast or protection
  board over EPDM membrane, per DL 220/2008 / Portaria 1532/2008

**Distâncias de Segurança e Corredores de Acesso**
- ANPC (Autoridade Nacional de Proteção Civil) guidance and SCIE regulations require:
  - Minimum 1.0 m clear perimeter setback from all roof edges and parapet upstands
  - Minimum 0.9 m unobstructed access corridors between panel rows
  - Clear access to all mechanical equipment (HVAC, smoke vents, hatches)
- These requirements directly influence maximum PV capacity per roof area
- Membriko fire setback plan is produced as part of the solar integration specification
  and submitted to the client for review by their fire safety consultant if required

**Painéis como Produto de Construção**
- Solar modules must carry CE marking (Low Voltage Directive, EMC Directive)
- IEC 61215 / IEC 61646 module qualification required
- RTIEBT compliance required for all electrical installation works
- Membriko coordinates membrane fire classification documentation into the overall
  fire safety dossier (Processo de Segurança Contra Incêndio) when required by the
  building's use category

**Visual:** Aerial roof plan diagram showing fire setback zones, access corridors and
panel layout within the compliant area. Regulatory badge grid.

## Section 9: Manutenção da Cobertura Sob os Painéis

**Purpose:** Reassure clients that post-installation maintenance is feasible and that
Membriko provides a structured plan.

**Content:**

**Inspecção Anual — O Que Inspeccionamos**
- Perimeter flashings, upstand connections and drains (accessible without panel removal)
- Visible seams in maintenance corridors between rows
- Any penetration seal at structural mounts
- Drainage performance: water must drain freely through corridors and to roof drains
- Evidence of biological growth on EPDM surface in shaded zones (harmless to EPDM
  but should be cleared from drains)

**Quando é Necessária Remoção de Painéis**
- Panel removal is only required if active water infiltration is detected whose source
  cannot be identified from accessible areas
- Membriko's electronic leak detection (post-installation) can localise leak points
  without panel removal in most cases, using capacitance-based scanning over accessible areas
- If panel removal is required, Membriko coordinates with the solar installer for
  temporary panel removal and replacement; electrical isolation required first

**Plano de Manutenção Documentado**
- Every Membriko solar integration project receives a written Plano de Manutenção:
  - Annual inspection scope and checklist
  - Procedures for emergency inspection if water infiltration is suspected
  - Contact protocol for coordinating solar installer involvement in membrane access
  - Record template for inspection findings

## Section 12: Legislação Solar em Portugal — O Que Precisa de Saber

**Purpose:** Demonstrate Membriko's knowledge of the full project context, not just roofing.

**Content:**

**DL 162/2019 — Autoconsumo de Energia Renovável**
- UPAC até 1 kWp: isento de registo
- UPAC 1-30 kWp: registo simplificado SERUP (5-10 dias úteis)
- UPAC 30-1.000 kWp: licenciamento completo; possível avaliação de impacto ambiental
- Compensação por injeção na RESP: 90% do preço médio OMIE do mês
- Prazo de registo: DGEG via portal SERUP

**Ligação à RESP (E-REDES / EDP Distribuição)**
- Qualquer instalação que possa exportar energia requer acordo de ligação com o operador
- Verificação da capacidade da rede de baixa tensão na proximidade do imóvel
- Contador bidirecional obrigatório para UPAC com ligação à rede

**Licenciamento Urbanístico**
- Edifícios existentes: maioria das instalações residenciais/comerciais enquadra-se em
  "obras isentas de licença" ou "comunicação prévia" ao abrigo do RJUE
- Edifícios em ARU (Área de Reabilitação Urbana) ou protegidos: consulta prévia obrigatória
  à Câmara Municipal; possível parecer DRCC
- Condomínios: aprovação em assembleia de condóminos necessária antes de qualquer instalação

## Section 14: FAQ — Perguntas Frequentes

**Purpose:** Capture long-tail search intent and reduce pre-sales enquiries.

Eight unique questions:

1. **A membrana EPDM suporta o peso do sistema de montagem solar por lastro?**
   Yes — ballasted solar mounts impose 15-25 kg/m² of roof area, less than conventional
   gravel ballast (50-80 kg/m²). Structural verification of the roof slab is still required;
   Membriko coordinates with a structural engineer as part of the project specification.

2. **Os painéis solares danificam a membrana EPDM durante a instalação?**
   The mounting system never contacts the EPDM membrane directly. Isolation pads (EPDM or
   neoprene) are placed under every mount foot. Membriko supervises the first day of mount
   installation and provides the isolation pad specification to the solar installer. Post-
   installation, Membriko inspects all accessible membrane areas before sign-off.

3. **Posso instalar painéis solares sobre uma membrana EPDM existente?**
   Yes, subject to a membrane condition survey. Membriko assesses the existing EPDM: if
   it is in serviceable condition, solar can proceed with Membriko documenting the pre-
   installation membrane state. If repairs are needed, Membriko completes them and re-tests
   before giving clearance for solar installation. Proceeding with solar over a degraded
   membrane is the costliest possible decision.

4. **O EPDM afecta negativamente a eficiência dos painéis solares?**
   EPDM is standard black, which absorbs some diffuse radiation that TPO white membranes
   would reflect back onto panel undersides. The production gain from white membrane
   reflection is real but marginal: typically 1-3% additional yield. EPDM's 50+ year
   service life eliminates a 25-30% production loss event (membrane replacement during
   panel life) — an incomparably larger advantage.

5. **Que distância de separação é necessária entre filas de painéis para manutenção?**
   SCIE and ANPC guidance requires minimum 0.9 m access corridors between panel rows on
   commercial buildings. Membriko's installation specification includes a coordinated panel
   layout plan respecting all fire setback distances, access corridors and drainage paths.
   This plan is produced before any work begins.

6. **Qual a inclinação mínima da cobertura para instalar painéis solares com EPDM?**
   Membriko recommends a minimum 1.5% fall for solar roofs (vs the general 1% minimum per
   RGEU). The increased fall recommendation compensates for drainage disruption caused by
   panel rows collecting and concentrating rainwater. Slope correction screeds can be
   applied to existing flat roofs with inadequate falls.

7. **A garantia Membriko mantém-se com painéis solares instalados?**
   Yes — Membriko's 20-year installation warranty applies to EPDM roofs with solar panel
   systems, subject to the solar installer following the isolation pad specification Membriko
   provides and not penetrating the membrane without prior written approval. All exclusions
   and conditions are documented in writing at project handover.

8. **Quanto tempo demora o processo de impermeabilização antes da instalação solar?**
   For a typical 200-500 m² commercial flat roof starting from bare concrete deck:
   3-7 working days for the EPDM installation and testing phase, plus 1-2 days for the
   coordination inspection after solar mount installation. For retrofits over existing
   membranes, add 2-5 days for membrane assessment and any repairs. Membriko provides
   a written programme at quotation stage.
