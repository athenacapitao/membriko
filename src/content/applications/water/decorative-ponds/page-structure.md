# Decorative Ponds and Ornamental Lakes — Page Structure

## Strategic Rationale

Decorative ponds and ornamental lakes represent one of the most emotionally resonant applications
for EPDM waterproofing. The reader is not buying a technical product — they are protecting a
garden that may have taken years to establish, a koi collection that represents a significant
investment, or a historic water feature tied to the identity of a property. The persuasion
architecture must speak to all three audiences simultaneously:

1. **Private homeowners and quinta owners** who want a pond that works for decades without liner
   failure and fish losses
2. **Hotel and resort managers** responsible for ornamental water features that must function
   reliably through the commercial season and withstand maintenance staff with pressure washers
3. **Landscape architects and garden designers** who need a specification-grade liner they can
   recommend with confidence and back with a warranty

The page opens with the cultural resonance of water in Portuguese garden tradition, then moves
immediately to the primary fear — liner failure and fish losses — to establish that Membriko
understands the stakes. It then builds the technical case with a structure that mirrors the
swimming pools page but is distinct in emphasis: fish safety, ecological neutrality, root
resistance and naturalisation technique (rather than chlorine resistance and regulatory compliance).

FAQs capture the most common search queries from koi keepers, garden builders and landscape
professionals — terms that are longer, more specific and often unserved by existing Portuguese
web content.

## Section 4: Fish and Aquatic Plant Safety

**Purpose:** Address the #1 concern for pond keepers: "Is the liner safe for my fish and plants?"
This section must be definitive, specific and reassuring — it is the page's most important
emotional conversion point.

**Content:**
- The plasticiser problem with PVC: phthalate and adipate plasticisers migrate into warm pond
  water; endocrine disruption in fish at concentrations achievable in small, warm, summer ponds;
  European Chemicals Agency (ECHA) classification of phthalates as substances of very high concern
- EPDM contains no plasticisers: inherently and permanently fish-safe; no degradation pathway
  that produces toxic migration products
- Migration data: pond-grade EPDM migration testing (ACS/WRAS protocols) shows no detectable
  organic compound migration at 23°C; sub-0.5 mg/L TOC at 60°C (far above pond water temperature)
- Koi and ornamental fish: 30+ years of koi-keeping community experience in EPDM-lined ponds,
  including breeding ponds, with no liner-related health incidents documented
- Concrete pH comparison: new concrete ponds require 4-8 weeks conditioning before fish
  introduction; EPDM-lined ponds are ready within 48-72 hours of filling
- Aquatic plants: EPDM provides no plant-toxic compounds; aquatic plants establish normally in
  EPDM-lined ponds, including sensitive species (Ranunculus aquatilis, Callitriche spp.)
- Root resistance of EPDM: EN 13948 (FLL) tested; water iris (Iris pseudacorus), bulrush
  (Typha latifolia) and water lily (Nuphar lutea) roots do not penetrate EPDM; PVC pond liner
  is not root-tested and fails at plant penetration points

## Section 5: Installation Process (7 Steps)

**Purpose:** Make the installation process feel systematic, professional and predictable.

**Content:** Numbered timeline.

1. **Visita Técnica e Diagnóstico** — On-site assessment of the pond site or existing feature;
   measurement of all dimensions; assessment of existing liner condition and substrate; evaluation
   of topography, drainage and groundwater conditions; inventory of all penetrations (bottom
   drains, skimmers, pipework)
2. **Projecto de Dimensionamento e Layout** — Cut plan designed to minimise seams; specification
   of membrane thickness (1.0 mm or 1.5 mm) and width; design of edge finishing technique;
   selection of penetration accessories for all bottom drains, skimmers and pipework
3. **Preparação do Substrato** — Removal of existing liner if required; substrate grading to
   eliminate sharp stones and roots; installation of geotextile underlay where substrate is
   angular or stony; shaping of marginal shelves and pond profile
4. **Instalação da Membrana EPDM** — Membrane placement beginning at the deepest point;
   careful draping to conform to pond geometry; full-surface adhesive bonding in critical
   zones (bottom, waterline band, edge termination); seam bonding with QuickSeam tape and
   primer where panels must be joined
5. **Pontos Singulares e Acessórios** — Installation of flanged EPDM accessories for all
   bottom drains, skimmers, pipework penetrations and electrical conduits; compression plate
   bolted seals on all penetrations
6. **Terminação e Naturalização do Bordo** — Edge finishing executed to agreed design: stone
   and cobble overlay, turf edge, timber decking overhang or planted marginal shelf; all
   terminations anchored to prevent uplift during heavy rainfall events
7. **Inspecção, Enchimento e Garantia** — Visual inspection of all seams and penetrations;
   gradual filling with level monitoring; 48-hour post-fill inspection; photographic as-built
   record; issue of 20-year Membriko installation warranty certificate

**Content:**

- Raised and above-ground ponds: timber-framed or brick-built raised ponds are lined with EPDM
  clamped at the top of the frame; the membrane supports the hydrostatic load without mechanical
  reinforcement when properly sized

## Section 7: Water Quality Advantages with EPDM

**Purpose:** Explain how the liner material actively contributes to better water quality in
ornamental ponds — a benefit that is often overlooked in liner comparisons.

**Content:**
- Chemically inert surface: EPDM adds nothing to the water; no lime leaching (concrete), no
  plasticiser release (PVC), no paint film degradation products (epoxy coatings)
- Biofilm compatibility: EPDM supports healthy biofilm establishment (beneficial bacteria that
  contribute to biological filtration); the surface is not bacteriostatic and does not require
  periodic chemical sterilisation
- pH stability: EPDM has no effect on water pH; freshwater pond pH is determined by the water
  source, fish load, plant activity and dissolved CO2 — not by the liner
- Algae management: EPDM's dark surface (black or dark grey) reduces light penetration to the
  pond bottom, limiting the substrate available for benthic algae growth; this is a supplementary
  benefit in addition to the primary filtration and plant competition mechanisms

## Section 9: Edge Finishing and Naturalisation

**Purpose:** Demonstrate Membriko's practical knowledge of the aesthetic outcome — the liner
detail that makes or breaks the natural appearance of an ornamental pond.

**Content:**
- Why edge finishing matters: the liner edge is the most visible element; a poorly terminated
  liner ruins the natural appearance of even a well-designed pond
- Techniques Membriko executes:
  - **Stone and cobble overlay:** river cobbles or dressed stone bedded on the EPDM flap;
    natural appearance; allows marginal plant establishment between stones
  - **Turf and grass edge:** liner extended under turf; low-maintenance; requires sufficient
    EPDM flap width (minimum 600 mm beyond waterline) to prevent turf slip
  - **Timber deck overhang:** hardwood decking overhangs 50-100 mm; clean, contemporary
    aesthetic; liner terminates under deck framing
  - **Planted marginal shelf:** gravel-filled shallow shelf planted with Phragmites, Iris,
    Typha; plants establish over liner flap within one season
  - **Formal coping stone:** sawn limestone, granite or ceramic coping mortared on a concrete
    upstand; liner terminates behind coping with mechanical bar anchor
- Waterfall and stream integration: EPDM continuous from pond to stream to waterfall face;
  stone overlay on stream bed conceals liner; Membriko coordinates stone laying to prevent
  sharp stone edges from bearing on membrane

## Section 11: Maintenance

**Purpose:** Show that EPDM ponds require substantially less maintenance than alternatives.

**Content:**
- Routine maintenance: none specific to the liner; standard pond water management (filtration,
  water quality monitoring, plant pruning) applies; no liner-specific treatments required
- Inspection: annual visual inspection recommended at the waterline band and all penetration
  points; check that stone and cobble edge overlay has not shifted to expose liner
- Cleaning: EPDM can be pressure-washed (up to 150 bar) and scrubbed with non-metallic brushes;
  standard pond algaecides (copper sulphate, QAC formulations) are fully compatible
- Repair: EPDM patch repair with QuickSeam tape and primer; small punctures can be patched
  while pond is partially drained to expose the damaged area; no specialised contractor required
  for simple patch repairs (technique provided in Membriko maintenance guide)
- What to avoid: sharp tools during pond clearance and plant division; metal-blade pond vac
  attachments that contact the liner; garden fork intrusion during marginal planting maintenance

## Section 14: FAQ (8 Questions)

**Purpose:** Capture long-tail search intent from ornamental pond owners, koi keepers and
landscape professionals.

1. **O EPDM é seguro para peixes — koi, goldfish, peixe-vermelho?**
   Yes — EPDM contains no plasticisers or biocides that migrate into the water. Koi have been
   kept in EPDM-lined ponds for 30+ years without liner-related health problems. PVC pond liner,
   by contrast, releases phthalate plasticisers into warm water; these are classified as endocrine
   disruptors by ECHA and can affect fish reproduction. Membriko recommends EPDM for all
   fish-bearing ornamental ponds.

2. **As raízes das plantas aquáticas podem perfurar a membrana EPDM?**
   No. EPDM is certified root-resistant to the FLL standard and EN 13948 — the same standard
   applied to EPDM used as root barriers in green roof construction. Water lily rhizomes, iris
   roots and bulrush roots cannot penetrate the membrane. PVC pond liner has no equivalent root
   resistance certification and is vulnerable to root penetration over time.

3. **Posso forrar um lago de betão antigo com EPDM sem o demolir?**
   Yes — this is one of the most common ornamental pond renovation scenarios. EPDM's 400%+
   elongation bridges the existing cracks in the concrete without compromise; the membrane
   provides a continuous, fish-safe waterproofing layer over the old concrete surface. The
   concrete does not need to be watertight — it only needs to be structurally stable and free
   of sharp projections that could puncture the new liner.

4. **Qual a espessura certa de EPDM para um lago ornamental?**
   For garden ponds and ornamental features, Membriko specifies 1.0 mm for small features
   (stream channels, water walls, features under 5 m²) and 1.5 mm for all ponds and lakes.
   For large public or hotel lakes exceeding 200 m² with heavy maintenance activity (pressure
   washing, wading access), 2.0 mm may be specified. The correct thickness depends on the
   specific project — the Membriko technical visit determines the appropriate specification.

5. **Quanto custa forrar um lago ornamental com EPDM?**
   Cost depends on the area, shape complexity, edge finishing specification and accessibility
   of the site. As an indicative range, EPDM pond lining by Membriko (including materials,
   installation, edge finishing and 20-year warranty) is typically positioned between the
   cost of professional concrete pond construction and periodic PVC liner replacement over
   20 years. The 50+ year service life means the lifecycle cost is significantly lower than any
   alternative. Contact Membriko for a free site-specific quotation.

6. **Posso instalar eu próprio o EPDM no meu lago?**
   For small garden ponds (under 10 m²), DIY EPDM installation is feasible for a careful
   homeowner using the correct materials and following best-practice guidance. For larger ponds,
   ponds stocked with high-value fish, ponds with complex geometry or multiple penetrations,
   and any project where a 20-year warranty is required, Membriko recommends professional
   installation. The edge termination and penetration sealing are the steps most critical to
   long-term performance and the most commonly done incorrectly in DIY installations.

7. **Qual a cor de EPDM disponível para lagos ornamentais?**
   Pond-grade EPDM is available in black and dark grey. No white or light-coloured EPDM pond
   liner is commercially available because the carbon black that provides UV resistance is also
   responsible for the dark colour. In practice, the dark liner colour enhances the visual depth
   of the water and creates an attractive mirror effect that landscape architects and garden
   designers actively favour. The water appears dark and natural rather than the artificial blue
   of a painted concrete pond.

8. **Qual a garantia que a Membriko oferece para lagos e tanques ornamentais?**
   Membriko provides a 20-year installation warranty on all ornamental pond and lake lining
   work, supplementary to the EPDM membrane manufacturer's product warranty. The warranty covers
   all seams, penetration details and edge terminations executed by Membriko. It is delivered
   as a formal warranty certificate at project handover, with photographic as-built documentation.
   The 5-year statutory defects liability period under the Portuguese Civil Code (Art. 1225)
   also applies to all Membriko installations.
